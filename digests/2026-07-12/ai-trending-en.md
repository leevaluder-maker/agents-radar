## 3. Trend Signal Analysis

The most explosive signal today is the **massive community adoption of agent skill frameworks and MCP servers** that give AI coding agents real operating system access. `DesktopCommanderMCP` (+909 stars) and `obra/superpowers` (+740 stars) are not just popular — they represent a paradigm shift where developers want their AI agents to act as **full-fledged OS-level operators**, not just chat completions. This is directly connected to the maturation of Claude Code, Codex, and Gemini CLI as serious development tools.

A second clear trend is the **rise of persistent memory and context management** as a critical infrastructure layer. Projects like `claude-mem` (86,873 stars), `mem0` (60,631 stars), and `cognee` (27,583 stars) are solving the fundamental limitation of agents that forget between sessions. The industry is realizing that without memory, agents cannot evolve or maintain long-running workflows.

Third, **knowledge graph-based RAG** is emerging as the next evolution beyond vector-only retrieval. `Graphify-Labs/graphify` (82,456 stars) transforms codebases and documents into queryable graphs, enabling AI to understand relationships between code, schema, and infrastructure — a major step toward true code comprehension.

Notably, the `stitch-skills` library from Google Labs signals that **open standards for agent skills** are becoming a priority. The Agent Skills open standard aims to make skills portable across Claude Code, Gemini CLI, Cursor, and others — reducing fragmentation in the agent ecosystem.

Finally, the appearance of `testtimescaling.github.io` and `awesome-llm-unlearning` suggests the research community is increasingly focused on **test-time compute scaling** and **model unlearning** — two emerging directions that will influence next-generation LLM deployment.
